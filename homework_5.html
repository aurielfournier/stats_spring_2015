I choose two two schemes with binomial weights because I think there might be some impact of the number of neighborhoods on the crime in a given neighborhood. I choose two with rook contigruity because neighborhoods that share sides may be more directly influenced then by those which share vertices. This first morans plot is looking at Queen contigruity with Binomial Neighbors. The points in the upper left and lower right quadrants are close to the mean, while the points in the upper right and lower left quadrants are farther away from the mean, and have four of the five outliers. This suggests that there is clustering, since the points which are away from the mean are also more like their neighbors (high residuals are near other high residuals).

The hotspot scores for both of the rook contigruity measures are the same 5.7337755. The queen contigruity is higher 10.6193902 (by about 100%) implying that under queen contigruity they are more clustered. This makes more sense since there is more information being pulled in, so they should be more clustered under a queen scenario.

Regardless of the contigruity the clustering is positive suggesting that crime is not randomly distributed across the city, polygons are more likely to have crime rates like their neighbors.
